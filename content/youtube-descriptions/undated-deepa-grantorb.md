I sat down with Deepa (founder at GrantOrb) to talk about AI-powered grant writing for creators. Deepa got into Web3 through a Polygon founder's COVID relief campaign and saw a massive gap where grant writers struggled to communicate ideas clearly on Gitcoin - that's where GrantOrb was born.

We walk through how Deepa built GrantOrb using fine-tuned LLMs to automate grant proposals, turning a time-consuming 30-minute manual process into just 5 minutes. She explains her model: a free outline preview so you can see the magic, then paid proposal generation for full access. Her team is building a grant scout agent that proactively finds grants tailored to your profile instead of you drowning in endless grant lists. Deepa breaks down her agentic architecture where multiple specialized LLMs handle individual tasks - one for forms, one for eligibility checking, one for writing - instead of one big model losing context halfway through. She's bootstrapped the whole thing and is growing through word of mouth.

For The ZAO musicians, this is phenomenal - no more procrastinating on grant proposals, no language barriers, just describe your project and let the agent handle it. This removes a ton of friction from the grant process so artists can focus on making music instead of fighting with foundation forms. Whether it's Canada Council of Arts, local Mural Festivals, or Web3 grants, Deepa's tooling makes it possible to hit way more opportunities with the same effort.
