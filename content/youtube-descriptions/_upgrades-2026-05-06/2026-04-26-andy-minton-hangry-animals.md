BCZ YapZ Episode 18 w/ Andy Minton from Hangry Animals

BCZ YapZ Episode 18 w/ Andy Minton (Hangry Animals): Web3 gaming for good, charity-backed subscriptions, and a live Unity demo of a stacking food-delivery racer.

I sat down with Andy "Hangry Andy" Minton, founder of Hangry Animals and Hang Studio. 27 years in the creative trenches as a creative director, animation studio owner, and agency lead before he jumped ship in 2019 to build for himself. Web3 found him through RFI (Reflect, the first reflective token, the code SafeMoon copied before going to jail), and the community pulled him in.

Andy walks through how Hangry Animals went from "Angry Hippos" (a Hungry Hippos NFT riff) to a 5-character collection (hippo, moose, zebra, bear, gorilla) drawn live on Twitch, then to the real vision: a gaming-for-good platform where subscription revenue funnels donations to animal welfare, mental health, and food poverty charities the community votes on. He breaks down the trust problem (charities run from Web3 unpredictability) and his answer: Web3 as a layer in the stack, not the whole meal. Follow the fun first.

Then the screen share. Andy demos the Unity prototype of the food-delivery racer: stack sushi and pizzas on your bike, deliver before the timer, dodge bombs that wipe your stack, drones that nick items mid-ride, and unibo enemies that turn into delivery guides. Toon-shaded Jet Set Radio aesthetic targeting tweens. Stacking physics, freeze-time pickups, climbing-spider crawlers driven by old-school AI movement, all rigged in Blender. USPTO trademarked across merch + games + entertainment. Hang Studio agency funds the build.

For The ZAO musicians and indie builders watching: this is what slow-and-resilient looks like. Andy didn't blow the budget early, didn't show too much too soon, and kept the IP playable across games, comics (on Crypto Comics), and animated series down the road. Just back from Games for Change at the London Games Festival.
