BCZ YapZ Episode 15 w/ Nikoline Arns from Hubs Network

Welcome back to another BCZ YapZ. This week I sit down with Nikoline of the Hubs Network, a growing global network of physical web3, coordination, and maker spaces that are building new ways for communities to actually work together.

Nikoline got her start as a designer in Barcelona, co-founded a school of 200 people running sociocracy from day one, then rolled into the web3 world through Akasha Foundation and the Akasha Hub. Today she is helping connect Akasha Hub, Commons Hub in Vienna, Liminal Village outside Rome, and a growing list of hubs across Europe and beyond so they can share tools, funding models, and knowledge instead of depending on big grants.

We talk about sociocracy as a fractal governance framework, why individualism in the US makes peer-to-peer coordination harder, the Plural Events format that runs the same conversation across 12+ cities on the same day, and the next one on May 14th. She also drops outcomes of collaborations with Radical Exchange, Web3 Privacy Now, and Logos, plus their upcoming panel at the Berlin neo-cyberpunk privacy summit.

If you build IRL community, care about governance that actually works, or want to host your own plural conversation, this one is for you.

Topics:
- Sociocracy vs holacracy as a governance framework
- The Hubs Network and how physical hubs support each other
- Plural Events: 12+ cities, one conversation, many cultures
- Akasha Hub, Nasha Hub, Commons Hub, Liminal Village
- Tools like Polis, Agora Citizen, and Context Engine
- Starting a hub in Maine
