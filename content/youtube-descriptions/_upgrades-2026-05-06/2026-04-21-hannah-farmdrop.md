BCZ YapZ Episode 17 w/ Hannah from Farm Drop

For this week's BCZ YapZ I got to sit down with Hannah from Farm Drop here in Downeast Maine. Farm Drop is a producer-driven local food network that has been running since 2011, connecting farms and fisheries across Blue Hill, Ellsworth, and surrounding communities through a volunteer-powered delivery model that just will not die because too many people depend on it.

Hannah is a fifth generation summer person in Blue Hill who grew up in Barcelona, studied at College of the Atlantic, and spent time with subsistence farmers in Mexico and Guatemala before diving into international food business in Germany. She brings a wild blend of global food systems experience to a very local operation.

We talk about the Food for Health program that pools community donations to stock pantries and meal sites, how Farm Drop built technology that eases collaboration without replacing the human network, and why the model works even when the business math does not quite pencil out. She walks through a packed upcoming calendar: May 9 community solutions at Reversing Falls Sanctuary in Brooksville, May 20 Grocery List documentary at the Ellsworth Library, a May 24 Soul Vendors fundraiser, and an August 6 supporter event at Sassafras Cafe.

If you care about local food, community resilience, or models of mutual aid that actually scale, Hannah is running one of the best examples in the region.
